### About this app

The PTX Business Opportunity Analyser (PTX BOA) is an interactive web application designed to assess production costs as well as greenhouse gas emissions of various hydrogen-based products across different countries.  

The analysis starts with the use of natural gas in the export country to produce products such as methanol, hydrogen, steel, iron, e-fuels, or ammonia. Users can calculate the delivered cost of molecules from an export country to an import country, including a detailed cost breakdown that highlights the competitive edge of one country against another.

The app includes a default dataset for all covered countries. This dataset guides the user and provides a high-level overview using pre-feasibility level information, while also allowing the user to manually adjust all relevant variables to accommodate local inputs. 

### Quick guide

- The **tab navigation bar** below the app title lets you navigate between different sections displaying results and data.
- In the **sidebar** on the left-hand side of the screen, you can change all important settings. The sidebar can be resized or completely hidden, which is especially useful on smaller screens.

##### What to find where?

Below is an overview of the available tabs and their contents:

- **Info:** (this page) General information about the app and its underlying methodology.
- **Costs:** Compare the costs of producing different molecules and exporting them to different countries.
- **Emissions:** Compare greenhouse gas emissions associated with the production and export of different molecules to different countries.
- **Input data:** View and modify the data used in the calculations.
- **Cost comparison to renewable-based products:** Compare production costs of natural gas based products with those based on renewable electricity.

Monetary units are denominated in 2025 US dollars (USD).

##### General hints on how to use the app

- **Figures are interactive:** Click on legend entries to hide or show individual elements. Hover over chart elements to display additional information.
- **Data tables are interactive:** Each table includes controls in the top-right corner for maximizing the view, searching within the table, or downloading the data as a CSV file.
- Additionally, most tables include a button below them that allows you to export the data as an Excel file.

##### Editing input data

Most of the input data used in the calculations can be edited. To enable editing, toggle the **data edit mode switch** at the bottom of the sidebar. Once activated, you can modify values in the tables on the **Input data** tab.

At the top of the screen, you will see a summary of all changes made, along with options to download your modifications or upload a previously saved changes file.

If input data has been modified, the **Costs** and **Emissions** tabs allow you to visualize results based on:
- the original dataset,
- the modified dataset, or
- the difference between the two.

You can revert all inputs to their defaults by turning off data edit mode again.

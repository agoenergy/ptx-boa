Choose the technology chain that converts natural gas into the final product you have chosen. The conversion can include several steps. 

The following abbrivations are used:

- ATR: Autothermal Reformer to produce hydrogen from natual gas including a carbon capture rate of 94%
- SMR: Steam Methane Reforming to produce hydrogen from natural gas applying partial carbon capture to process emissions.
  CO₂ is captured from the PSA tail gas resulting in a carbon capture rate of 53%. Capturing CO₂ from syngas or tail gases is the most common setting in currently operating facilities.
  Modelling studies show that capturing CO₂ from both process and combustion and achieving capture rates over 90% is possible, but increases capital cost and required energy input substantially.
- SMR Brownfield: Adds a carbon capture unit to an existing SMR plant, resulting in low CAPEX costs for hydrogen production
- GtL: Gas to liquid
